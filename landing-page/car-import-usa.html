IMPORT PATHWAYS

The Right Scheme,
For Your American Car

Importing from the USA operates under multiple Australian government pathways. Picking the wrong one is the most common — and most expensive — mistake. Here's how we match your American car to the right scheme.

SECTION 36 · RVSR 2019

The 25-Year Rule

Vehicles 25 years or older (measured by build date, not model year) qualify for import under concessional entry. Perfect for classic Mustangs, first-gen Broncos, C10 pickups, square-body Chevys and pre-2001 American collectables.

  • VIA processing typically 3–4 weeks
  • Passenger cars (MA), light goods (NA), motorcycles, tricycles
  • No RAWS modification required for non-road or pre-reg use
25+ years old
SEVS REGISTER

Specialist & Enthusiast Vehicle Scheme

For modern American performance, rare, or unique vehicles meeting one of six eligibility criteria — think Mustang GT, Corvette, Shelby & Hellcat. All SEVS imports must be processed by a Registered Automotive Workshop (RAW).

  • Performance: >110 kW/tonne (pre-2020) or >130 kW/tonne (2020+)
  • Rarity: fewer than 3,000 units/year per make
  • Environmental, mobility, LHD-original & campervan criteria
SEVS Register
PERSONAL IMPORT SCHEME

For Expats & Returning Residents

Australian citizens, PRs and eligible visa holders returning from the US who've owned their vehicle for 12+ months can import it under the Personal Import option — one vehicle per five years.

  • 10% GST applies; duty & LCT may apply by value
  • Continuous 12-month ownership & use overseas required
  • Concessions available on compliance requirements
Personal Import

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to import a car from the USA to Australia?

As a guide, sea freight from US ports averages around 30 days, and VIA processing typically takes 3–4 weeks (these can overlap). Add compliance and delivery time on arrival. We'll provide a realistic, end-to-end estimate during your quote based on your car, the chosen scheme and the departure port.

Can I import a left-hand-drive car from the USA?

Yes, in many cases. LHD vehicles 25 years or older can be kept left-hand drive under the 25-year rule, while newer cars are assessed under SEVS or the Personal Import Scheme — some may require conversion to comply. We confirm exactly what applies to your vehicle before you buy.

Are there hidden costs?

No. We provide transparent pricing and explain all potential costs upfront — including US port handling, freight, marine insurance, customs duty, 10% GST, Luxury Car Tax (if applicable), asbestos testing, compliance work and delivery.

Do you handle everything?

Yes — from US collection and approvals to shipping, compliance and Australian delivery. We're a licensed Customs & Quarantine broker with bonded depots in Sydney, Brisbane, Melbourne and Fremantle.

What about asbestos testing?

Australia has a zero-tolerance asbestos policy, and older American vehicles can carry asbestos in brakes, gaskets and clutches. Testing is mandatory and costs roughly $800–$1,000 if done locally. We can coordinate offshore testing before shipment to save you time and money.

Which US ports do you ship from?

We consolidate and ship from every major US port, including Los Angeles, Houston, Savannah and New York/New Jersey — via shared container for valuable, modified or low-clearance cars, or RORO for standard road vehicles. We recommend the right method and port per vehicle.
