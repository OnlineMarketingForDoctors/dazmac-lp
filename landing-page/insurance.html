General Information About Imported Vehicle Insurance

Imported vehicles can differ from locally delivered vehicles in areas such as:

  • Availability of parts
  • Repair costs
  • Modifications or specifications
  • Compliance requirements

Because of these differences, insurers may assess imported vehicles differently from standard vehicles.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is specialist vehicle insurance?

It is cover designed for vehicles that fall outside the standard market, such as imported, prestige, performance, classic and modified cars. Specialist insurers understand these vehicles, value them correctly and offer features like agreed value and repairer choice that mainstream policies often do not.

What is agreed value cover?

Agreed value means you and the insurer settle on the insured value of your vehicle when the policy starts. If the car is written off, you are covered for that agreed figure rather than a general market estimate. This is especially important for rare imports and appreciating classics.

Can I insure a modified or imported vehicle?

Yes. Specialist underwriting expects modifications and unique specifications rather than treating them as a problem. There are a number of insurers available that cater to imported, prestige, modified and specialist vehicles, including MB Insurance Group.

Can I choose my own repairer?

Repairer choice is one of the core benefits. You can keep the trusted specialist workshop that knows your vehicle, rather than being directed to a default network.
