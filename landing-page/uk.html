IMPORT PATHWAYS

The Right Scheme,
For Your British Car

Importing from the UK operates under multiple Australian government pathways. Picking the wrong one is the most common — and most expensive — mistake. Here's how we match your British car to the right scheme.

SECTION 36 · RVSR 2019

The 25-Year Rule

Vehicles 25 years or older (measured by build date, not model year) qualify for import under concessional entry. Perfect for Jaguar E-Types, classic Minis, MGs, Triumphs, early Land Rovers and pre-2001 British collectables.

  • VIA processing typically 3–4 weeks
  • Passenger cars (MA), light goods (NA), motorcycles, tricycles
  • No RAWS modification required for non-road or pre-reg use
25+ years old
SEVS REGISTER

Specialist & Enthusiast Vehicle Scheme

For modern British performance, rare, or unique vehicles meeting one of six eligibility criteria — think McLaren, Lotus, Aston Martin & performance saloons. All SEVS imports must be processed by a Registered Automotive Workshop (RAW).

  • Performance: >110 kW/tonne (pre-2020) or >130 kW/tonne (2020+)
  • Rarity: fewer than 3,000 units/year per make
  • Environmental, mobility, RHD-original & campervan criteria
SEVS Register
PERSONAL IMPORT SCHEME

For Expats & Returning Residents

Australian citizens, PRs and eligible visa holders relocating from the UK who've owned their vehicle for 12+ months can import it under the Personal Import option — one vehicle per five years.

  • 10% GST applies; duty & LCT may apply by value
  • Continuous 12-month ownership & use overseas required
  • Concessions available on compliance requirements
Personal Import

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to import a car from the UK to Australia?

As a guide, sea freight from UK ports averages around 40 days, and VIA processing typically takes 3–4 weeks (these can overlap). Add compliance and delivery time on arrival. We'll provide a realistic, end-to-end estimate during your quote based on your car, the chosen scheme and the departure port.

Do I need to convert the steering to right-hand drive?

No. The UK drives on the left and British cars are right-hand drive — the same as Australia — so there's no steering conversion required. This makes the UK one of the most straightforward markets to import from.

Are there hidden costs?

No. We provide transparent pricing and explain all potential costs upfront — including UK port handling, freight, marine insurance, customs duty, 10% GST, Luxury Car Tax (if applicable), asbestos testing, compliance work and delivery.

Do you handle everything?

Yes — from UK collection and approvals to shipping, compliance and Australian delivery. We're a licensed Customs & Quarantine broker with bonded depots in Sydney, Brisbane, Melbourne and Fremantle.

What about asbestos testing?

Australia has a zero-tolerance asbestos policy, and older British vehicles can carry asbestos in brakes, gaskets and clutches. Testing is mandatory and costs roughly $800–$1,000 if done locally. We can coordinate offshore testing before shipment to save you time and money.

Which UK ports do you ship from?

We consolidate and ship from every major UK port, including Southampton, London Gateway, Felixstowe and Liverpool — via shared container for valuable, modified or low-clearance cars, or RORO for standard road vehicles. We recommend the right method and port per vehicle.
